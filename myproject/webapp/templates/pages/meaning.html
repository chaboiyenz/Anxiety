Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a psychotherapeutic approach that focuses on identifying and changing negative thought patterns and behaviors to alleviate emotional distress and improve overall mental health. It involves collaboration between the therapist and the individual to challenge and reframe unhelpful beliefs, develop coping strategies, and implement positive behavioral changes.

Defusion Technique

Defusion techniques are cognitive strategies used in acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT) to help individuals distance themselves from unhelpful thoughts, beliefs, and emotions. These techniques involve observing thoughts without judgment, recognizing them as passing mental events rather than absolute truths, and using metaphors or humor to reduce their impact and influence on behavior.

Turning to Religion

Turning to religion involves seeking solace, guidance, and meaning through spiritual beliefs, practices, and community involvement during times of difficulty, uncertainty, or personal struggle.
